HOW TO READ THIS DOCUMENT
-------------------------------------------------------------------------------
This is a design reference, not a tutorial. Sections 1–3 give the thesis and the
evidentiary basis for every decision that follows — read them first, because the
architecture is a direct consequence of published failure data, and skipping the
"why" makes the "what" look arbitrary. Sections 4–11 are the system design proper
(components, data models, control flow, memory). Sections 12–13 cover the concrete
build and cost. Sections 14–17 cover failure mitigation, validation, and a phased
roadmap. Section 18 is the reference list so every claim is traceable.

Design maxim carried throughout (Anthropic, "Building Effective Agents"):
    "Add complexity only when it demonstrably improves outcomes."
This system is complexity. It must earn its place against a simpler baseline on
YOUR tasks before it ships. Section 15 is how you prove that.


===============================================================================
 1. EXECUTIVE SUMMARY / THESIS
===============================================================================

1.1 WHAT AGORA IS
AGORA is a deliberation engine: a bounded set of specialized "expert" agents that
argue and discuss a question, grounded against real evidence, adjudicated by a
separate judge, and scored on a calibrated warrant scale. It is designed for
technical decisions in Python backend and AI/ML development — architecture
choices, design/code review, RAG and eval design, inference/deployment tradeoffs,
security review — where the output is a *reasoned, evidence-backed decision*, not
a code artifact produced by parallel agents.

1.2 THE CORE THESIS: DELIBERATE WIDE, EXECUTE NARROW
The central architectural commitment, forced by the field's two most credible
production teams converging in 2025–2026:
    - Deliberation is multi-agent. Diversity of grounded perspective plus
      aggregation plus verification is where multi-agent genuinely helps.
    - Execution is single-threaded. One executor implements the accepted
      decision. Multiple agents writing/implementing in parallel is where
      multi-agent reliably FAILS (Cognition, "Don't Build Multi-Agents").
This split (a "brain/hands" decoupling) preserves the benefit and removes the
dominant failure mode. See §2.6 and §3.1.

1.3 NON-NEGOTIABLE PRINCIPLES (the "iron hooks")
    P1  Every claim carries provenance. A claim is TOOL_RESULT, RETRIEVED_SOURCE,
        EXTERNAL_CITATION, or ASSERTION. Assertions cannot exceed a warrant floor.
    P2  Confidence is DERIVED, never self-reported. No agent's raw "73%" is
        trusted (LLMs are systematically overconfident; §2.3).
    P3  The judge is a separate non-participant. Debaters do not score debates
        they are in (conflict of interest; Irving et al. 2018 debate model).
    P4  Full traces are shared among debaters; context is never silently
        partitioned (Cognition Principle 1).
    P5  Roles are crisp and non-overlapping. Duplicate/ambiguous roles are the
        single largest failure category (MAST; §2.4).
    P6  Termination and budget are explicit and hard-capped. "Missing termination
        conditions" is a named MAST failure mode; cost compounds ~10x on runaway.
    P7  Memory compounds insight without compounding error. Encoded conclusions
        remain challengeable; a mistakes ledger has equal standing (§10).
    P8  In this domain, most disagreements are empirically resolvable. Prefer
        running an experiment over running another debate round (§3.2, §11).


===============================================================================
 2. RESEARCH FOUNDATIONS (the evidentiary basis)
===============================================================================
Each finding below is stated with the design constraint it imposes. Full
citations in §18.

2.1 MULTI-AGENT DEBATE IS MOSTLY AGGREGATION WEARING A COSTUME
Du et al. (ICML 2024) showed multi-agent debate improves factuality/reasoning.
Huang et al. (2310.01798) replicated it and found it only marginally beats
self-consistency at equal sample count, and UNDERPERFORMS simple majority voting
for the same number of responses; they argue it is better understood as
"consistency," not "debate." A 2026 analysis attributes much of the benefit to
aggregation alone.
=> CONSTRAINT: The value is not the arguing. It is (a) diverse grounded evidence,
   (b) aggregation, (c) external verification. Do not assume debate > voting;
   PROVE it on your tasks (§15). If it doesn't win, keep this architecture but
   swap the debate core for a voting core.

2.2 HETEROGENEITY HELPS; HOMOGENEOUS CLONES DO NOT
Adaptive Heterogeneous Multi-Agent Debate (A-HMAD) reports 4–6% absolute accuracy
gains over standard debate and >30% fewer factual errors from DIVERSE specialized
agents vs. homogeneous ones.
=> CONSTRAINT: Distinct expert roles with distinct tools and priors — not N copies
   of one prompt. This is the justification for the roster in §5.

2.3 LLM CONFIDENCE IS SYSTEMATICALLY OVERCONFIDENT AND LOW-RESOLUTION
FermiEval (2510.26995): nominal 99% confidence intervals covered truth ~65% of
the time. Verbalized confidence is overconfident irrespective of correctness, and
"saturates" — collapsing to coarse values like 0.9/1.0. Mitigations that work:
aggregation across independent samples, proper scoring rules (Brier), CoT-
ensembling (ECE reduced up to ~88% in one study).
=> CONSTRAINT: Never elicit and trust a fine-grained percentage. Derive scores
   from agreement + evidence + independent judge; bucket coarsely; calibrate with
   proper scoring rules over time (§7).

2.4 MOST MULTI-AGENT FAILURES ARE DESIGN, NOT MODEL
MAST — "Why Do Multi-Agent LLM Systems Fail?" (Cemri et al., 2503.13657, NeurIPS
2025): 1,600+ annotated traces across 7 frameworks (Cohen's kappa 0.88). Gains
over single agents are often minimal. 14 failure modes in 3 buckets:
    - Specification / system-design issues (~42%): task misinterpretation,
      ambiguous role definitions, duplicate agent roles, poor decomposition,
      MISSING TERMINATION CONDITIONS.
    - Inter-agent misalignment (~37%): ignoring peer input, acting on incorrect
      assumptions, derailment.
    - Task verification (~21%): weak/absent verification, premature termination.
Even deliberately EASY coding tasks fail. The fix is system design, not bigger
models.
=> CONSTRAINT: This taxonomy is the checklist the architecture is built against.
   §14 maps every bucket to a concrete mechanism.

2.5 CONVERGENCE/SYCOPHANCY IS THE REAL DANGER, NOT PERSISTENT DISAGREEMENT
Peer exposure induces Asch-style conformity; debate is weakened by premature
agreement and consensus-seeking. Agents can converge while LOSING factual content
and viewpoint diversity — a "deliberative illusion" final-answer metrics miss.
Without external feedback, LLMs often DEGRADE answers on reconsideration.
=> CONSTRAINT: Reward warranted, preserved disagreement; treat rapid agreement as
   SUSPECT. Anonymize speaker identity. Ground every reconsideration in a tool,
   not in peer pressure (§7.4, §8).

2.6 THE SINGLE-VS-MULTI WAR RESOLVED INTO A SYNTHESIS
Cognition ("Don't Build Multi-Agents", 2025): partitioned context makes parallel
subagents produce conflicting implicit decisions (the Flappy Bird example — a
Mario background + a non-game-asset bird), forcing a costly reconciliation step;
so share full traces and keep writes single-threaded. Anthropic ("How we built
our multi-agent research system"): a lead + parallel subagents beat a single agent
by 90.2% on their eval — but at ~15x the tokens, only worth it for high-value
breadth-first tasks. By 2026 BOTH converged on role-scoped subagents under a
coordinator, shared context, single-threaded writes. A SWE-bench leaderboard
analysis found architecture (single vs multi) is NOT the deciding variable.
=> CONSTRAINT: §1.2 (deliberate wide, execute narrow) + §3.4 (share full traces).

2.7 THE CANONICAL REFERENCE IMPLEMENTATION IS A TOY (verified by reading it)
composable-models/llm_multiagent_debate (Du et al.): 825 lines total; the debate
loop is ~40 lines that concatenate peers' prior answers into the next prompt.
Hardcoded 3 agents / 2 rounds. No judge, no tools, no retrieval, no scoring, no
termination logic; the main loop ends on a pdb.set_trace() breakpoint. It is a
research artifact.
=> CONSTRAINT: There is no off-the-shelf base to inherit. Judge, evidence gating,
   scoring, termination, and memory are all net-new and are the actual product.

2.8 ANTHROPIC PLATFORM PRIMITIVES YOU CAN BUILD ON
    - Agent SDK subagents: fresh isolated context, tailored system prompt, scoped
      tool allowlist, optional per-agent model. Subagents cannot spawn subagents.
      The parent↔subagent channel is only the prompt string + the returned final
      message. For many-agent, script-driven orchestration there is a Workflow
      mechanism that runs outside the conversation.
    - Agent Skills: a folder with SKILL.md (YAML frontmatter: name + description).
      Progressive disclosure in 3 tiers — metadata (~tens of tokens/skill) loaded
      at startup; full SKILL.md loaded on match; bundled reference files loaded
      only when needed. Anthropic explicitly recommends capturing "successful
      approaches and common mistakes" into skills — this IS the deepening mechanic
      (§10).
    - Reported gap: the SDK ships no built-in cross-session state, durable
      execution, observability, or multi-agent coordination beyond subagents-as-
      tools. You build those (§12).

2.9 PRIOR ART IN SOFTWARE-AGENT ROLE DESIGN
MetaGPT (SOP-driven PM/Architect/Engineer + shared memory pool; but rigid roles,
resource hallucinations referencing non-existent files/classes, costly GPT-4 call
cascades, heavy asyncio). ChatDev (waterfall chat-chain; propagates early errors).
MAGIS (Manager/Repository-Custodian/Developer/QA; ~8x over raw GPT-4 on SWE-bench
issue resolution — a locator role + a reviewer role is a productive split).
=> CONSTRAINT: Borrow the "locator + reviewer" split and shared memory; avoid rigid
   waterfalls and unguarded code execution (§11 safety).


===============================================================================
 3. DESIGN PRINCIPLES (distilled invariants)
===============================================================================

3.1 DELIBERATE WIDE / EXECUTE NARROW (from §2.6)
The deliberation graph may run many agents. The moment a decision must be turned
into changes, exactly one Executor acts, carrying the full deliberation verdict as
context. No parallel writers.

3.2 EVIDENCE-GATED CLAIMS (from §2.1, §2.5, §2.7)
No claim rises above WARRANT floor "WEAK" without evidence. In this domain,
"evidence" is preferentially a resolver-tool artifact: a green test, a reproduced
eval number, a benchmark measurement. Retrieval and external citation are second
tier. Bare assertion is capped.

3.3 DERIVED, NOT ELICITED, CONFIDENCE (from §2.3)
Scores are computed from inter-sample/agent agreement, evidence strength, and an
independent judge rubric. Coarse 5-band scale. Proper scoring rules used to
calibrate over time.

3.4 SHARE FULL TRACES; ANONYMIZE IDENTITY (from §2.5, §2.6)
Debaters see each other's full reasoning (prevents talking-past-each-other), but
see peers under stable per-session anonymous handles, not role labels (blunts
authority-driven conformity).

3.5 CRISP, NON-OVERLAPPING ROLES (from §2.4)
Every role has a one-line mandate, a unique "what it uniquely catches," and a
distinct tool allowlist. Two roles that would catch the same class of problem are
merged or one is cut.

3.6 EXPLICIT TERMINATION + HARD BUDGET (from §2.4, §2.6)
Convergence, marginal-gain, max-rounds, and budget stops are all defined and
enforced by the Orchestrator, independent of any agent's opinion.

3.7 MEMORY THAT COMPOUNDS INSIGHT, NOT ERROR (from §2.5, §2.8)
Conclusions, open disagreements, and mistakes are persisted with warrant tags.
Conclusions remain challengeable; a wrong past consensus must be supersedable so
"deeper next session" does not mean "deeper into a hole."

3.8 MEASURE BEFORE COMPLEXITY (from §2.1, §2.4, and the design maxim)
The whole system is gated behind beating a single-agent + self-consistency
baseline on a real eval set (§15).

4.2 COMPONENT RESPONSIBILITIES (summary; details in later sections)

  ORCHESTRATOR (§9) — Mechanical control. Owns the state machine, round structure,
    disagreement routing, budget accounting, circuit breakers, termination. Has NO
    opinion on the question. Model tier: mid (cheap; it does routing, not
    reasoning) or plain code where deterministic.

  DEBATER POOL (§5) — The expert agents. Each: scoped mandate, distinct tools,
    produces Claims with provenance. Sees full anonymized traces of peers.

  JUDGE / VERIFIER (§5, §6, §7) — Separate non-participant. Assigns warrant bands,
    classifies disagreements, gates uncited claims, produces the scored verdict.
    Model tier: frontier.

  SYNTHESIZER (§5) — Neutral final write-up: the decision, the warrant per claim,
    and PRESERVED disagreements. May be the Architect role wearing a neutral hat,
    but never the Judge.

  EXECUTOR (§5) — Single agent that implements the accepted decision, runs tests,
    reports results as new evidence. Only component that writes to the target repo/
    environment.

  EVIDENCE / CLAIM BUS (§6) — The shared substrate carrying all Claims and traces.
    Anonymization applied here.

  RESOLVER TOOLS (§11) — Sandbox, test runner, eval harness, profiler, retrieval.
    Turn empirical disagreements into measurements.

  MEMORY (§10) — Skills + three ledgers in Qdrant/pgvector. Loaded at framing,
    written at session end.


===============================================================================
 5. THE AGENT ROSTER (role charters)
===============================================================================
Roster is intentionally tight. More agents = more tokens (15x already) AND more
convergence risk (§2.5). Start with the META roles + a 5-role debate core + the
Executor; add roles only if an eval shows a missed failure class.

Every charter has: MANDATE | UNIQUELY CATCHES | TOOLS | MODEL TIER | BOUNDARY.
Each agent's system prompt is a versioned artifact (small changes to a lead prompt
can unpredictably shift subagent behavior — keep them under version control and
diff them in evals).

--- META ROLES (not debaters) ---

ORCHESTRATOR
  MANDATE: Run the protocol. Decompose the task, assign roles, sequence rounds,
    route disagreements, enforce budget/termination.
  UNIQUELY CATCHES: runaway loops, budget overrun, missing termination.
  TOOLS: state store, budget meter, dispatch (no reasoning tools).
  MODEL TIER: mid / deterministic code.
  BOUNDARY: never asserts a position on the question.

JUDGE / VERIFIER
  MANDATE: Assign warrant bands to claims; classify each disagreement; gate
    uncited claims; emit the scored verdict.
  UNIQUELY CATCHES: unwarranted claims dressed as facts; the 21% verification gap.
  TOOLS: read-only access to Claim bus + Evidence store; may re-run a resolver to
    confirm a claimed result (trust-but-verify).
  MODEL TIER: frontier.
  BOUNDARY: does not generate positions; only evaluates them. Not a debater.

SYNTHESIZER
  MANDATE: Produce the neutral decision write-up with per-claim warrant and
    preserved disagreements.
  UNIQUELY CATCHES: false consensus (it must report disagreements, not smooth
    them over).
  TOOLS: read-only Claim bus + verdict.
  MODEL TIER: frontier.
  BOUNDARY: adds no new claims; reports only adjudicated ones.

--- DEBATE CORE (recommended 5) ---

SOFTWARE ARCHITECT
  MANDATE: System design, boundaries, data flow, coupling, interface contracts,
    long-term evolvability.
  UNIQUELY CATCHES: structural mistakes, leaky abstractions, wrong seams.
  TOOLS: retrieval (docs/prior decisions), diagramming, code read.
  MODEL TIER: frontier.
  BOUNDARY: owns design shape, NOT operational runtime (SRE) or shipping-cost
    (Pragmatist).

QA / CORRECTNESS
  MANDATE: Correctness, edge cases, contract adherence, test adequacy.
  UNIQUELY CATCHES: logical defects, uncovered edge cases, missing tests.
  TOOLS: test runner, code sandbox, retrieval.
  MODEL TIER: mid.
  BOUNDARY: correctness only, not performance (Empiricist/SRE) or security
    (Hacker).

RESEARCH SCIENTIST (ML/AI)
  MANDATE: Modeling choices, algorithmic soundness, novelty, statistical validity
    of evals, literature grounding.
  UNIQUELY CATCHES: unsound ML methodology, invalid eval design, ignored prior art.
  TOOLS: retrieval (papers/docs), eval harness, code sandbox.
  MODEL TIER: frontier.
  BOUNDARY: soundness/novelty, NOT production operability or shipping speed.

HACKER (OFFENSIVE SECURITY)
  MANDATE: Attack surface, auth/authz weaknesses, injection (incl. prompt
    injection into this very system), supply-chain, data exfiltration paths.
  UNIQUELY CATCHES: exploitable weaknesses others rationalize away.
  TOOLS: code sandbox (isolated), retrieval (CVE/advisories), static analysis.
  MODEL TIER: frontier.
  BOUNDARY: adversarial exploitation, distinct from a defensive threat-model
    checklist (fold defense into QA or add a dedicated role only if evals show a
    gap). NOTE: this role reasons about vulnerabilities to harden the system; it
    does not produce weaponized exploit code — findings are described and verified
    in the sandbox, and remediation is the deliverable.

SRE / OPERATIONS
  MANDATE: What breaks in production — failure modes, observability, rollback,
    blast radius, latency/throughput under load, on-call reality.
  UNIQUELY CATCHES: operational failure the Architect (design elegance) and QA
    (correctness) both miss.
  TOOLS: profiler/benchmark, load simulation, code read, retrieval.
  MODEL TIER: mid.
  BOUNDARY: runtime operability, not design shape or research soundness.

--- OPTIONAL DEBATE ROLES (add only if evals justify) ---

EMPIRICIST / MEASUREMENT
  MANDATE: Refuse claims lacking a benchmark or reproduction; own the "run the
    experiment" instinct. In AI/ML this is the enforcer of P8.
  UNIQUELY CATCHES: hand-wavy performance/quality claims.
  TOOLS: eval harness, profiler, code sandbox.
  MODEL TIER: mid.
  BOUNDARY: demands measurement; does not itself argue design/security.
  NOTE: If you keep the roster tight, the Empiricist FUNCTION can be enforced by
  the Judge's provenance gate rather than a separate agent. Prefer that first.

PRAGMATIST / SHIPPING
  MANDATE: YAGNI, maintenance cost, deadline reality, reversibility of decisions.
  UNIQUELY CATCHES: gold-plating and over-engineering by Architect/Scientist.
  TOOLS: retrieval, code read.
  MODEL TIER: mid.
  BOUNDARY: cost-to-ship and maintainability, not correctness or security.

--- EXECUTOR ---

EXECUTOR
  MANDATE: Implement the accepted decision as changes; run tests; report results.
  UNIQUELY CATCHES: n/a (it acts, it does not debate).
  TOOLS: full write access to target repo/env (isolated), test runner, sandbox.
  MODEL TIER: frontier.
  BOUNDARY: SINGLE INSTANCE. Never run parallel Executors on the same target.


===============================================================================
 6. CLAIM & EVIDENCE MODEL (the data structures)
===============================================================================
This is the heart of the "iron hooks." Everything an agent says becomes a Claim
with mandatory provenance; the Judge gates on it.

6.1 CLAIM

  Claim {
    id:            uuid
    round:         int
    author_role:   enum(ARCHITECT, QA, SCIENTIST, HACKER, SRE, EMPIRICIST,
                        PRAGMATIST)        # visible to Judge/Orchestrator only
    author_anon:   string                  # stable per-session handle shown to peers
    statement:     string
    claim_type:    enum(EMPIRICAL, DEFINITIONAL, NORMATIVE, PROCEDURAL)
    provenance:    enum(TOOL_RESULT, RETRIEVED_SOURCE, EXTERNAL_CITATION, ASSERTION)
    evidence:      [EvidenceRef]           # MUST be non-empty unless provenance=ASSERTION
    warrant_band:  enum(CONTESTED, WEAK, MODERATE, STRONG, VERIFIED)  # set by JUDGE
    depends_on:    [claim_id]
    contradicts:   [claim_id]
    status:        enum(OPEN, SUPPORTED, REFUTED, SUPERSEDED)
  }

6.2 EVIDENCE REFERENCE

  EvidenceRef {
    kind:   enum(TEST_RUN, EVAL_RESULT, BENCHMARK, RETRIEVAL_HIT, WEB_SOURCE,
                PRIOR_LEDGER, STATIC_ANALYSIS)
    ref:    string          # sandbox run id / eval id / vector doc id / url / ledger id
    digest: string          # short hash or summary for audit + dedup
    metric: optional { name:string, value:number, unit:string, ci:optional }
  }

6.3 PROVENANCE TAXONOMY AND THE WARRANT CEILING (Judge policy)

  provenance = TOOL_RESULT      -> eligible for up to VERIFIED
  provenance = RETRIEVED_SOURCE -> eligible for up to STRONG
  provenance = EXTERNAL_CITATION-> eligible for up to STRONG (if source authoritative)
  provenance = ASSERTION        -> CAPPED at WEAK (reasoning-only, never "fact")

The Judge assigns the actual band <= the ceiling based on corroboration and
contradiction. A claim contradicted by a TOOL_RESULT drops to CONTESTED/REFUTED
regardless of how confidently it was stated.

6.4 THE GATE
Before any claim influences the verdict, the Judge verifies:
  - provenance present and consistent with evidence,
  - evidence refs resolve (the run/doc actually exists; optionally re-run),
  - no silent contradiction with a higher-warrant claim.
Claims failing the gate are marked WEAK/CONTESTED and cannot anchor a decision.


===============================================================================
 7. CONFIDENCE & SCORING MODEL
===============================================================================
Directly implements §2.3. The percentage the user wants exists, but it is an
OUTPUT of measurement, never an INPUT from an agent.

7.1 WHY NOT ELICITED PERCENTAGES
LLM verbalized confidence is overconfident irrespective of correctness and
saturates to 0.9/1.0. A crisp elicited "73%" is false precision on noise.

7.2 THE FIVE WARRANT BANDS (the scale)
  VERIFIED  — reproduced by a resolver tool (green test / eval number / benchmark).
  STRONG    — multiple independent authoritative sources, no contradiction.
  MODERATE  — single source or strong reasoning, not independently reproduced.
  WEAK      — reasoning-only, plausible, unverified (ceiling for pure assertion).
  CONTESTED — active unresolved disagreement, or refuted by evidence.

7.3 DERIVED CLAIM SCORE
  score(claim) = combine(
      agreement:  fraction of INDEPENDENT samples/agents converging on it,
      coverage:   the assigned warrant_band,
      judge:      independent judge rubric score
  )
Report the BAND, not a raw number. If a numeric score is required downstream,
compute it and CALIBRATE it with a proper scoring rule (Brier / log-loss) against
resolved outcomes accumulated over sessions (§10 ledgers provide ground truth).
Optionally ensemble multiple independent reasoning passes before scoring (the
CoT-ensembling result: large ECE reductions).

7.4 THE AGREEMENT SIGNAL, REDEFINED (implements §2.5)
Do NOT treat "high agreement = good." Track the PAIR (agreement_level, mean_warrant):

  low agreement  + low warrant  -> EPISTEMIC GAP     -> dispatch resolver tools;
                                                        get evidence, don't re-argue.
  low agreement  + high warrant -> GENUINE TRADEOFF  -> PRESERVE both; escalate to
       (both sides)                                     human; this is a value call.
  high agreement + low warrant  -> SUSPECT (possible  -> inject a challenge / require
                                   sycophancy or         tool-backed evidence before
                                   deliberative          accepting. Do NOT trust.
                                   illusion)
  high agreement + high warrant -> CONVERGED          -> terminate this thread.

This turns "why do agents disagree" into an explicit, actionable classification,
and correctly makes false consensus — not persistent disagreement — the thing the
system distrusts.


===============================================================================
 8. DISAGREEMENT MODEL
===============================================================================

8.1 DISAGREEMENT OBJECT

  Disagreement {
    id:               uuid
    claim_a, claim_b: claim_id
    dclass:           enum(EMPIRICAL, DEFINITIONAL, VALUE_TRADEOFF, ERROR)  # Judge-set
    route:            enum(DISPATCH_RESOLVER, OPERATIONALIZE, ESCALATE_HUMAN, ADJUDICATE)
    status:           enum(OPEN, RESOLVED, PRESERVED)
    resolution:       optional EvidenceRef
  }

8.2 CLASS -> ROUTE (the routing table the Orchestrator executes)

  EMPIRICAL      (a fact settles it)        -> DISPATCH_RESOLVER
       Run the test/eval/benchmark. The disagreement collapses to a measurement.
  DEFINITIONAL   (terms used differently)   -> OPERATIONALIZE
       Force both parties to define the term operationally, then re-check. Most
       "productive-looking" fights are actually this.
  VALUE_TRADEOFF (no fact resolves it)      -> ESCALATE_HUMAN
       e.g. latency vs cost vs time-to-ship. Surface the tradeoff explicitly with
       each side's warrant; do NOT manufacture consensus. status -> PRESERVED.
  ERROR          (one side is simply wrong) -> ADJUDICATE
       Judge rules against the evidence. status -> RESOLVED.

8.3 DEADLOCK POLICY
An OPEN VALUE_TRADEOFF is not a failure to be looped on. It escalates once and is
reported PRESERVED. Never spend rounds trying to collapse a genuine value call.


===============================================================================
 9. ORCHESTRATION & CONTROL FLOW
===============================================================================

9.1 STATE MACHINE

  INIT
   -> LOAD_MEMORY        (retrieve topic ledgers from Qdrant/pgvector; §10)
   -> FRAME              (decompose task; select roster; set budget B_max, R_max)
   -> GROUND_INDEP       (round 0: each debater grounds via tools, NO peer view)
   -> CRITIQUE           (shared-trace round: agents critique anonymized peers)
   -> JUDGE              (assign warrant bands; classify disagreements)
   -> ROUTE              (per §8.2, for each OPEN disagreement)
        -> RESOLVE       (dispatch resolver tools; attach EvidenceRef)
        -> OPERATIONALIZE
        -> ESCALATE_HUMAN
        -> ADJUDICATE
   -> CONVERGENCE_CHECK  (§9.3)
        -> (not converged AND budget remains) -> CRITIQUE     [loop]
        -> (converged OR budget/round cap)     -> SYNTHESIZE
   -> SYNTHESIZE         (neutral verdict + preserved disagreements)
   -> EXECUTE?           (optional: single Executor implements; runs tests)
   -> PERSIST_MEMORY     (write conclusions / open disagreements / mistakes)
   -> DONE

  Cross-cutting guards (can fire from any state):
   - BUDGET_GUARD    -> force SYNTHESIZE when tokens/time near cap.
   - CIRCUIT_BREAKER -> ABORT on runaway (e.g. resolver recursion, tool storm).

9.2 ROUND STRUCTURE
Round 0 is independent grounding with NO peer visibility (preserves diversity;
prevents anchoring — cf. §2.5). Rounds 1..R_max are shared-trace critique with
anonymized peers. Judge runs every round. Resolvers run on demand between rounds.

9.3 TERMINATION CONDITIONS (all enforced by Orchestrator, agent-opinion-independent)
  CONVERGENCE:   agreement >= theta AND mean_warrant >= MODERATE AND no OPEN
                 EMPIRICAL disagreements.
  MARGINAL_GAIN: delta(mean_warrant) over the last round < epsilon (diminishing
                 returns -> stop).
  MAX_ROUNDS:    round == R_max.
  BUDGET:        tokens >= B_max OR wall_clock >= T_max.
  DEADLOCK:      only OPEN disagreements remaining are VALUE_TRADEOFF -> escalate
                 + synthesize (do not loop).

9.4 BUDGET ENFORCEMENT & CIRCUIT BREAKERS (implements §2.4/§2.6 cost reality)
  - Global token budget B_max per run; per-role sub-budgets.
  - Max agents, max rounds, max resolver invocations per run.
  - Resolver recursion depth = 0 for subagents (subagents cannot spawn subagents).
  - "Marginal evidence gain" stop: if a round adds no new VERIFIED/STRONG claims,
    do not fund another round.
  - Task-value floor: if estimated cost > configured value ceiling for the task
    class, REFUSE to run the full loop; fall back to single-agent (§13).


===============================================================================
 10. MEMORY & CROSS-SESSION DEEPENING
===============================================================================
Implements the "each session goes deeper" requirement via the mechanism Anthropic
explicitly recommends: capture successes and mistakes into skills. Backed by the
existing RAG store.

10.1 THE THREE LEDGERS
  CONCLUSIONS        — decisions reached, each with warrant band + evidence digest.
  OPEN_DISAGREEMENTS — unresolved value tradeoffs and epistemic gaps to revisit.
  MISTAKES           — dead-ends, refuted claims, resource hallucinations, wrong
                       past consensus. EQUAL STANDING to conclusions (§3.7).

10.2 LEDGER ENTRY

  LedgerEntry {
    topic_key:     string          # coarse routing key (e.g. "rag.chunking",
                                   #  "inference.ondevice", "auth.hce")
    kind:          enum(CONCLUSION, OPEN_DISAGREEMENT, MISTAKE)
    statement:     string
    warrant_band:  enum(...)
    evidence_digest: string
    session_id:    uuid
    timestamp:     iso8601
    status:        enum(ACTIVE, CHALLENGED, SUPERSEDED)
    supersedes:    optional entry_id
  }

10.3 STORAGE & LOADING
  - Embed each entry; store in Qdrant/pgvector with structured metadata.
  - At FRAME, retrieve entries by topic_key + semantic similarity to the task.
  - Package durable, reusable lessons as a versioned Skill per topic
    (SKILL.md + references), so progressive disclosure surfaces only the relevant
    lessons cheaply. Metadata tier keeps many topics available at negligible token
    cost; full lesson text loads only on match.

10.4 PATH-DEPENDENCY GUARD (critical; implements §2.5/§3.7)
  - Conclusions are CHALLENGEABLE: a new session with higher-warrant evidence can
    set status=SUPERSEDED and write a superseding entry.
  - The Judge consults MISTAKES at FRAME; repeating a logged dead-end is flagged.
  - Never treat a prior CONCLUSION as VERIFIED unless it carries tool-backed
    evidence; otherwise it re-enters as MODERATE/WEAK and must be re-warranted.
  This prevents "deeper next session" from compounding an early wrong consensus
  (the deliberative-illusion failure extended across time).


===============================================================================
 11. EVIDENCE TOOLING (resolvers)
===============================================================================
The domain superpower: in Python backend + AI/ML work, most disagreements are
empirically checkable, so "proofs only" is actually enforceable. Resolvers turn
arguments into artifacts.

11.1 RESOLVER SET
  CODE SANDBOX    — execute arbitrary candidate code in isolation; return stdout/
                    stderr/exit + artifacts. (Docker/K8s isolation; §11.3.)
  TEST RUNNER     — run the project's test suite / targeted tests; green tests are
                    VERIFIED-tier evidence for correctness claims.
  EVAL HARNESS    — run a fixed eval set (retrieval recall, model accuracy, RAG
                    answer quality); reproduced numbers settle ML quality claims.
  PROFILER/BENCH  — measure latency/throughput/memory/cost under defined load;
                    settles performance/cost claims (e.g. ONNX on-device vs server).
  RETRIEVAL       — query Qdrant/pgvector for prior art, docs, and past ledgers;
                    RETRIEVED_SOURCE-tier evidence.

11.2 EVIDENCE CONTRACT
Every resolver returns a structured EvidenceRef (§6.2) with a digest and, where
applicable, a metric {name,value,unit,ci}. The Judge may re-run any resolver to
confirm a claimed result (trust-but-verify), guarding against fabricated evidence
(the "source fabrication" failure the better debate repos explicitly defend
against).

11.3 SAFETY (mandatory; learned from MetaGPT resource-hallucination failures and
      general agent risk)
  - Untrusted code executes ONLY in an isolated sandbox (no host FS, scoped
    network, resource limits, ephemeral). Your Docker/K8s handles this.
  - PROMPT INJECTION: content fetched by retrieval/web is DATA, not instructions.
    The Hacker role explicitly probes for injection into this system; the
    Orchestrator treats tool outputs as untrusted.
  - The Executor writes only to an isolated working copy; promotion to the real
    target is a separate, human-gated step for consequential changes.
  - Resolvers validate that referenced files/classes exist before use (prevents
    the MetaGPT-style "reference a non-existent asset -> execution fails" mode).


===============================================================================
 12. TECHNOLOGY STACK & BUILD
===============================================================================
Mapped to the existing stack: FastAPI, Celery/Redis, Qdrant/pgvector,
LangChain/LlamaIndex, PyTorch/ONNX, Docker/K8s, Claude/OpenAI APIs.

12.1 ORCHESTRATION LAYER — TWO SANE OPTIONS (pick one; the middle is wrong)

  OPTION A (recommended default): LangGraph for graph semantics + your existing
    FastAPI/Celery/Redis for execution.
    WHY: the deliberation is a stateful graph with cycles, conditional edges,
    human-in-the-loop checkpoints, and durable resumable state — LangGraph's exact
    sweet spot, with the best token control among the frameworks. Run the graph
    inside Celery workers; Redis holds durable job state; LangGraph checkpoints the
    deliberation state. Add LangSmith (or equivalent) for the tracing MAST demands.

  OPTION B: own the orchestration entirely on FastAPI + Celery + Redis + the
    Messages API. More upfront work; total control over the bespoke warrant/scoring
    state machine (which no framework models natively). Justified precisely because
    your "iron hooks" scoring is custom.

  AVOID as the backbone: CrewAI / AutoGen. Role-frameworks are awkward to bend into
    a deterministic state machine, and custom orchestration fights AutoGen's
    conversation assumptions. Borrow AutoGen's debate PATTERNS as reference; do not
    build the backbone on them.

12.2 AGENT RUNTIME
  - Use Claude Agent SDK subagents for research FAN-OUT inside a single role
    (fresh isolated context, scoped tools, optional per-agent model). Remember:
    subagents cannot spawn subagents; the only parent<->child channel is the
    prompt + returned message, so pass file paths/errors/decisions explicitly.
  - For many-agent, script-driven orchestration beyond a few delegations, use the
    SDK Workflow mechanism (runs outside the conversation context).

12.3 EVIDENCE + MEMORY
  - Qdrant/pgvector: dual role — evidence store (retrieval resolver) AND cross-
    session memory (ledgers/skills). Keep namespaces separate.
  - LlamaIndex/LangChain: ingestion, chunking, retrieval plumbing for the RAG
    resolver (reuse your production RAG pipeline).

12.4 EXECUTION / ISOLATION
  - Docker/K8s: sandbox for the code/test/eval/profiler resolvers and the Executor
    working copy. Ephemeral, resource-capped, network-scoped.

12.5 MODEL ROUTING (cost-shaped; from Anthropic's research-system economics)
  - Frontier tier (Opus-class): Judge/Verifier, Software Architect, Research
    Scientist, Synthesizer, Executor — where adjudication and consequential
    reasoning happen.
  - Mid tier (Sonnet-class): the general debater pool (QA, SRE, Pragmatist,
    Empiricist).
  - Small/fast tier (Haiku-class): research fan-out subagents, retrieval
    summarization, routine classification, the Orchestrator's mechanical steps.
  Pin TIERS in config, not exact model versions (versions churn; the architecture
  is version-agnostic). Measure where tokens actually go and re-tune.

12.6 OBSERVABILITY (non-optional; the SDK does not ship it and MAST requires it)
  - Trace every claim, tool call, judge decision, and state transition.
  - Retain deliberation transcripts (treat as logs with potentially sensitive
    data: scope access, set retention).
  - Instrument an LLM-as-judge failure annotator (§15) to classify traces by MAST
    mode over time and watch the failure distribution shift as you intervene.


===============================================================================
 13. COST MODEL & GUARDRAILS
===============================================================================

13.1 THE BASELINE REALITY
Multi-agent runs ~15x the tokens of a single chat, and the multiplier only pays
off for high-value tasks (Anthropic). This system is MORE than a fan-out — it
loops with resolvers — so treat 15x as a floor, not a ceiling.

13.2 COMPOUNDING RISK
Runaway (recursive spawning, oversized tool returns, infinite source-hunting) can
multiply cost ~10x further. The published multi-agent systems shipped WITHOUT
circuit breakers and hit "50 subagents for a simple question." You must add them
(§9.4).

13.3 HARD CONTROLS (config-driven)
  - B_max tokens / T_max wall-clock per run; per-role sub-budgets.
  - Max rounds R_max; max agents; max resolver invocations.
  - Marginal-gain stop (no new VERIFIED/STRONG claim -> no new round).
  - TASK-VALUE FLOOR: classify tasks by value; if projected cost exceeds the floor
    for that class, the Orchestrator REFUSES the full loop and runs single-agent +
    self-consistency instead. Cheap questions must never enter the 15x path.

13.4 COST ACCOUNTING
Attribute tokens per role and per resolver; surface a per-run cost report. Use it
to prune the roster (cut roles whose marginal contribution < their cost) and to
re-tune model tiers.


===============================================================================
 14. FAILURE MODES & MITIGATIONS (MAST-mapped)
===============================================================================
Every mitigation below maps to a mechanism defined earlier.

14.1 SPECIFICATION / SYSTEM-DESIGN (~42% of failures)
  Ambiguous/duplicate roles    -> crisp non-overlapping charters w/ distinct tools
                                  and explicit BOUNDARY lines (§5).
  Poor task decomposition      -> FRAME state decomposes before roster assignment
                                  (§9.1).
  MISSING TERMINATION          -> four explicit termination conditions enforced by
                                  the Orchestrator (§9.3).
  Task misinterpretation       -> operationalize definitional disagreements early
                                  (§8.2).

14.2 INTER-AGENT MISALIGNMENT (~37%)
  Ignoring peer input          -> shared FULL traces on the Claim bus (§3.4).
  Acting on wrong assumptions  -> provenance gate: assumptions capped at WEAK,
                                  cannot anchor decisions (§6.3).
  Authority-driven conformity  -> anonymized peer identity; suspect-flag on
                                  high-agreement/low-warrant (§7.4).
  Derailment / status spam     -> Orchestrator controls turn-taking; no free-form
                                  cross-talk (§9.2).

14.3 TASK VERIFICATION (~21%)
  Weak/absent verification     -> separate Judge/Verifier + resolver tools as
                                  ground truth (§5, §11).
  Premature termination        -> convergence requires mean_warrant >= MODERATE AND
                                  no OPEN empirical disagreements (§9.3).
  Fabricated evidence          -> trust-but-verify: Judge may re-run resolvers;
                                  EvidenceRef must resolve (§11.2).

14.4 DEBATE-SPECIFIC FAILURES
  Deliberative illusion /      -> reward preserved warranted disagreement; distrust
   stance homogenization          fast agreement; round-0 independent grounding
                                  (§7.4, §9.2).
  Degradation on reconsideration-> reconsideration must be evidence-driven (a
                                  resolver result), never peer-pressure-driven.
  Debate ~= voting (no lift)   -> gated behind the baseline comparison (§15); if no
                                  lift, swap to a voting core, keep the scaffold.

14.5 EXECUTION FAILURES
  Conflicting parallel writes  -> single-threaded Executor (§3.1).
  Resource hallucination       -> resolvers validate referenced files/classes
                                  exist; sandbox isolation (§11.3).
  Prompt injection             -> tool outputs treated as untrusted data; Hacker
                                  role probes injection paths (§11.3).


===============================================================================
 15. EVALUATION & VALIDATION PLAN
===============================================================================
This section is how you avoid building an expensive system that doesn't beat a
cheap one. It is the gate on the whole project.

15.1 THE BASELINE (build FIRST, before the full loop)
  - Single frontier agent + self-consistency (N independent samples, aggregate).
  This is the number the whole architecture must beat. If AGORA cannot beat it on
  your tasks, you do not have a case for the debate core.

15.2 THE EVAL SET
  - 30–50 REAL tasks from your backlog (backend + AI/ML decisions and reviews),
    with known-good outcomes or expert-graded rubrics. Cover the domains your roles
    claim to own (design, correctness, ML soundness, security, ops).

15.3 METRICS (measure all four; not just accuracy)
  - QUALITY: task accuracy / expert-rubric score vs baseline.
  - COST: tokens and dollars per task (the 15x must buy proportional quality).
  - CALIBRATION: Expected Calibration Error / Brier of the derived scores against
    resolved outcomes (are the warrant bands honest?).
  - AGREEMENT DYNAMICS: does the system converge appropriately, or exhibit
    false-consensus / thrash? Track (agreement, mean_warrant) trajectories.

15.4 THE DEBATE-VS-VOTE DECISION GATE
  Run: baseline (self-consistency) vs. voting-core AGORA vs. debate-core AGORA,
  controlling for token count. If debate does not beat voting at equal cost, ship
  the VOTING core inside this same architecture (Judge + resolvers + memory are
  independent of the core). Reference: "Debate or Vote" (NeurIPS 2025); Huang et
  al. (§2.1).

15.5 FAILURE-MODE TRACKING (continuous)
  - Run an LLM-as-judge annotator over deliberation traces to classify failures by
    MAST mode (the MAST paper ships a validated pipeline for this). Watch the
    distribution move as you intervene. Gate prompt/model changes on the eval set,
    not on vibes.

15.6 REGRESSION GATING
  - No role-prompt, model-tier, or protocol change ships without re-running the
    eval set. Version prompts; diff behavior across versions.


===============================================================================
 16. PHASED IMPLEMENTATION ROADMAP
===============================================================================
Each phase is gated: it must beat the previous phase on the §15 eval set before
proceeding. This operationalizes "measure before complexity."

  PHASE 0 — FOUNDATIONS
    - Build the eval set (§15.2) and the baseline (§15.1).
    - Stand up the resolver tools (sandbox/test/eval/profiler/retrieval) and the
      Evidence store. These are useful even to the baseline.
    - Deliverable: a reproducible number to beat.

  PHASE 1 — SINGLE-ROUND DELIBERATION + JUDGE + EVIDENCE GATING
    - 3–5 roles, one critique round, Claim model + provenance gate, Judge, warrant
      bands. No memory, no loop.
    - Gate: beats baseline on quality at acceptable cost.

  PHASE 2 — MULTI-ROUND + DISAGREEMENT ROUTING + TERMINATION
    - Add rounds, the disagreement classifier + routing table, convergence /
      marginal-gain / budget termination, circuit breakers.
    - Gate: quality up, cost bounded, no thrash.

  PHASE 3 — RESOLVERS AS FIRST-CLASS RESOLUTION
    - Wire empirical-disagreement -> dispatch-resolver so arguments become
      measurements. Judge trust-but-verify.
    - Gate: empirical disagreements resolve by evidence, not by more rounds.

  PHASE 4 — MEMORY & DEEPENING
    - Three ledgers + Skills packaging + path-dependency guard. Load at FRAME,
      write at end.
    - Gate: session N+1 measurably outperforms N on a repeated topic WITHOUT
      inheriting a wrong consensus (test with a seeded-error case).

  PHASE 5 — EXECUTOR & FULL LOOP
    - Single-threaded Executor implements accepted decisions, runs tests, feeds
      results back as evidence. Human gate on consequential promotion.
    - Gate: end-to-end decision->implementation->verification on real tasks.

  ONGOING — CALIBRATION & ROSTER TUNING
    - Accumulate resolved outcomes; calibrate scores (Brier). Prune roles whose
      marginal value < cost. Re-tune model tiers from the cost report.


===============================================================================
 17. OPEN QUESTIONS & RISKS (honest unknowns)
===============================================================================
  R1  Debate vs vote on YOUR tasks is unproven. Mitigation: §15.4 gate. This is
      the single biggest risk to the project's premise.
  R2  Who judges the Judge? A single frontier judge can be wrong or miscalibrated.
      Mitigations to evaluate: judge ensembles, calibrating judge scores against
      resolved outcomes, periodic human spot-audit of verdicts.
  R3  Calibration transfer: proper-scoring-rule calibration derived on one task
      distribution may not transfer to another. Re-calibrate per domain.
  R4  Cost ceilings in practice: real workloads may blow B_max often; the task-
      value floor policy needs tuning so useful runs aren't starved and trivial
      ones don't enter the 15x path.
  R5  Roster tuning is empirical: the "right" number of roles is workload-specific;
      start tight, expand only on demonstrated missed failure classes.
  R6  Anonymization vs. capability routing tension: hiding role identity from peers
      (anti-conformity) while the Orchestrator still routes by role — verify the
      anonymization actually holds end-to-end and isn't leaked in phrasing.
  R7  Prompt-injection through resolvers/retrieval is a live attack surface; the
      Hacker role's findings on the system itself must be acted on, not just noted.

 18. GLOSSARY & REFERENCES

18.1 KEY TERMS
  Warrant band     — the evidence-strength tier assigned to a claim by the Judge.
  Provenance       — the evidential origin of a claim (tool / retrieval / citation
                     / assertion) that ceilings its warrant.
  Resolver         — a tool that converts an empirical disagreement into a measured
                     artifact (test/eval/benchmark/retrieval).
  Deliberate wide / execute narrow — multi-agent for reasoning, single-agent for
                     implementation.
  Deliberative illusion — apparent consensus achieved by losing facts/diversity.
